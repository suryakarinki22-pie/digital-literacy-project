## Type of Crime: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
 
UPI fraud is a scam where criminals pretend to be bank employees and trick people into giving away their money. College students are one of the main targets because many of us are using UPI apps like PhonePe and Google Pay for the first time.
 
---
 
## How It Happened – Step by Step
 
**Step 1 – The Call:** A college student got a call from someone claiming to be an SBI customer care executive. He said the student's KYC was expired and the account would be blocked by evening. The student panicked.
 
**Step 2 – Building Trust:** The scammer already knew the student's name and last few digits of the account number, so the call felt genuine.
 
**Step 3 – The Trap:** The caller asked the student to download AnyDesk for "online KYC verification." The student installed it thinking it was an official process.
 
**Step 4 – Taking Control:** Once installed, the scammer could see everything on the student's screen and quietly sent a UPI payment request in the background.
 
**Step 5 – OTP Stolen:** An OTP arrived on the student's phone. The scammer read it through the screen share and approved the payment. Rs. 12,000 was gone within seconds.
 
**Step 6 – Too Late:** The student realized only after getting a debit message. He filed a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in but could not recover the money.
 
---
 
## Why It Works
 
The scammer never hacked anything. He just used fear and urgency to stop the student from thinking clearly.
 
---
 
## What Should Have Been Done
 
Simply hang up and call the bank's official number directly. Banks never ask for OTP or ask you to install any app over a call.
