# Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

## Part A – Coding Practice (HackerRank)

I created an account on HackerRank and completed two beginner 
level challenges from the Python Introduction section.

The first one was "Say Hello, World! With Python" which was 
basically just printing Hello World — simple but it got me 
familiar with how HackerRank works.

The second one was "Solve Me First" where I had to complete 
a function that returns the sum of two integers. Since I 
already know Python basics this wasn't difficult but it was 
good practice.

Screenshots of both completed challenges are included in 
this folder as proof.

## Part B – Google Workspace (Google Forms)

I created a Google Form titled "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" 
with 15 questions — 4 multiple choice and 1 short answer question.

The quiz covers topics like what digital literacy means, safe 
internet practices, cybercrime awareness, UPI safety and 
professional platforms for students.

The form is linked to a Google Sheet that automatically collects 
responses whenever someone fills it out.

## How These Tools Will Help Me Academically

HackerRank will help me practice coding regularly and prepare 
for placement tests in later years. Google Forms is something 
I can use for collecting survey data, creating quizzes for 
group projects and collaborating with batchmates on shared 
documents through Google Workspace.
